Who we are

A developing organisation, built on international standards

Assistance Dogs South Africa is a growing organisation focused on training highly skilled assistance dogs for individuals with disabilities and specialised support needs.

Our programs are designed around international standards and personalised support, ensuring every assistance dog team is trained safely, ethically, and effectively — while keeping our approach personal, supportive, and accessible.

Our Dogs

A dog trained for every kind of need

From guiding steps safely to interrupting anxiety before it takes hold — every dog is matched to the person they'll work alongside.

Guide Dogs

Supporting individuals with visual impairments to navigate safely and independently.

Autism Assistance Dogs

Emotional grounding, safety, routine, and behavioural interruption for ages 4 and up.

Psychiatric Assistance Dogs

Deep pressure therapy, anxiety interruption, grounding, and retrieval tasks.

Service Mobility Dogs

Retrieving, opening doors, carrying items, and getting help when needed.

Training Programs

Three pathways, one standard of excellence

Whichever path suits your lifestyle, every successful team qualifies as a reliable, task-trained assistance dog.

Owner-Trained Adult Dog

Best for clients who already own a suitable adult or rescue dog, aged roughly 12–18 months, pending suitability assessment.

Owner-Trained Puppy

Best for clients starting with a young puppy, working closely with an instructor over 18–24 months of development.

Organisation-Trained

Our most supported option — we source, raise, and train the dog, then guide a structured handover to you.
